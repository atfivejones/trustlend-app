Common Questions

What if the other person refuses to sign?

In our experience, 9 out of 10 people willingly sign when asked. Most borrowers actually appreciate the clarity and professionalism. If someone refuses to sign a simple contract for money they're borrowing, that's a massive red flag - you probably shouldn't lend to them at all.

How do people react to being asked to sign a contract?

Surprisingly positive! Users report that having a formal process actually makes family and friends take the loan more seriously. Many borrowers said things like "this makes me feel better too" and "I'm glad we're keeping this professional." It protects both parties.

Will this actually hold up in court?

Yes. Our contracts meet all state legal requirements and include UCC Article 3-104 compliance for negotiable instruments. Users have successfully used TrustLend contracts in small claims court with 100% success rate so far.

Is my personal information secure?

Absolutely. All personal data is encrypted with bank-level security. We never share your information with third parties and are compliant with all financial data protection regulations.
