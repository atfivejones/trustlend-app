Tips & Best Practices

1

Always Verify Borrower Identity

Use the ID verification feature (Maximum Protection) to confirm the borrower's identity before finalizing any agreement. This adds legal weight and reduces fraud risk.

2

Set Clear Payment Terms

Specify exact payment amounts, due dates, and consequences for late payment. Ambiguous terms can lead to disputes and make enforcement difficult.

3

Document Everything

Keep records of all communications, payment receipts, and changes to agreements. TrustLend automatically tracks many of these for you.

4

Use Graduated Reminders

Start with friendly reminders and gradually increase urgency. The smart reminder system (Maximum Protection) handles this automatically.

5

Consider Interest and Fees

Clearly state any interest rates or late fees in your contract. Ensure they comply with local laws and are reasonable.

6

Plan for Default Scenarios

Include provisions for what happens if payments are missed. Consider payment plans, collateral, or legal remedies.

7

Regular Account Monitoring

Check your dashboard regularly for overdue payments and contract status updates. Set up notifications to stay informed.

8

Backup Your Data

Regularly export your contract data and store copies securely. This protects you if you need to change platforms or for legal proceedings.
